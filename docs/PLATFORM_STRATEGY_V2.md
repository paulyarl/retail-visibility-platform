## Platform Architecture, Tier Framework & Organisational Model — V2
*Confidential Working Document*

> **V2 Change Summary:** Restructured tiers around **business model alignment** rather than feature stacking. Purchase capabilities (deposit vs. full) now match merchant business models (physical vs. online), preventing customer confusion at checkout.

---

# Platform Mission
> *Local retailers need a way to move shoppers from real-time product discovery through to purchase conversion because big-box retailers built end-to-end inventory visibility and commerce infrastructure internally — leaving small retailers without an accessible, connected, or affordable path to compete in mobile shopping.*

---

# The V2 Strategic Insight

## The Problem We Solved
**Tier-based features ≠ Merchant capabilities**

In the original model, a merchant in Tier 3 automatically got deposit checkout options. But if they're e-commerce only, deposits don't make sense for their business model. This created:

- **Customer confusion:** Deposit options from online-only merchants who can't fulfill them
- **Conversion friction:** Shoppers see options that don't match the merchant's reality
- **Merchant frustration:** Paying for features they can't actually use

## The V2 Solution
**Align tiers with business models, not just feature accumulation**

Each tier now specializes in a commerce type that matches how merchants actually operate:

- **Commitment Tier:** Deposit-only for physical retailers driving foot traffic
- **E-commerce Tier:** Full-payment only for online-only merchants  
- **Omnichannel Tier:** Both options for merchants with physical + online presence

This eliminates the merchant-customer mismatch while providing clear upgrade paths based on business evolution, not just feature accumulation.

# Pricing & Revenue Model

## Pricing Philosophy

Visible Shelf operates in an uncontested market with no natural competition. The engineering investment to replicate the platform represents a substantial capital and time barrier to entry. A free model would undervalue the platform, under-fund its development, and attract uncommitted retailers.

**Model: 14-day free trial across all tiers. Paid from day 15. No exceptions.**

## Tier Pricing

| Tier | Name | Price | Value Proposition |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Tier 1** | Discovery | $29 / month | Complete Google visibility stack. Search, Shopping, Maps & SWIS. |
| **Tier 2** | Storefront | $59 / month | Platform presence added. Branded store, product browsing, shopper inquiry. |
| **Tier 3** | Commitment | $79 / month | Deposit commerce for physical retailers. Drive foot traffic with guaranteed intent. |
| **Tier 4** | E-commerce | $99 / month | Full e-commerce for online merchants. Clean online checkout, no deposit confusion. |
| **Tier 5** | Omnichannel | $149 / month | Unified commerce. Shoppers choose deposit/pickup OR full payment/delivery. |
| **Tier 6** | Enterprise | $499 / month | Complete business solution. Multi-location, enterprise support, custom contracts. |

## Platform Revenue Streams

| Revenue Stream | Model | Detail |
|---|---|---|
| **Monthly subscription** | Recurring flat fee | All tiers — predictable, scalable revenue base |
| **Holding fee processing** | Small % per transaction | Applied to every Tier 3+ reservation commitment |
| **Abandoned fee share** | 20–25% of forfeited deposit | Retailer receives 75–80% — platform earns on protection service |
| **Online payment processing** | Transaction % on sales | Tier 4+ completed online payments |
| **Multi-location premium** | Per-location fee | Tier 6 Enterprise organisational accounts |

> The platform generates revenue whether the shopper shows up or not — fairly, transparently, and without penalising retailers.

# Organisational Model — Multi-Location

Multi-location businesses — chains, franchises, regional retailers — require a tier model that serves both the organisation as a whole and individual locations with flexibility. Visible Shelf supports two layers: the Organisation Account and the Individual Location.

## The Inheritance & Upgrade Model

```
Organisation Account sets the baseline tier (any of Tier 1–5)
↓
All locations automatically inherit baseline tier features
↓
Individual locations can upgrade above the baseline — never below it
↓
Organisation dashboard provides unified view across all locations and tiers
↓
Billing consolidated at organisation level
```

## Can an Organisation Be Any Tier?

Yes — any of the tiers, not just Tier 5.

| Org Tier | Organisation Type | Real-World Scenario |
|---|---|---|
| **Tier 1** | Google-first multi-location | Small chain wanting Google visibility across all locations with minimal investment. |
| **Tier 2** | Brand-presence organisation | Regional brand wanting a unified storefront presence across all locations. |
| **Tier 3** | Commitment commerce chain | Physical retail chain wanting reservation and commitment commerce capability deployed uniformly. |
| **Tier 4** | E-commerce organisation | Online-only multi-brand wanting unified e-commerce across all digital storefronts. |
| **Tier 5** | Omnichannel organisation | Full-scale multi-location retailer wanting end-to-end commerce across all locations. |

## Individual Location Flexibility Rules

| Rule | What It Means | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **Downward inheritance** | All locations receive org baseline features automatically | Org on Tier 2 → every location gets storefront and product visibility |
| **Upward flexibility** | Individual locations can upgrade above the org baseline | Org on Tier 2, flagship location upgrades to Tier 5 |
| **No downgrade below baseline** | A location cannot operate below the org tier | Org on Tier 3 → no location can drop to Tier 1 or 2 |
| **Unified billing** | Org pays baseline × locations + individual upgrade differentials | Tier 2 org × 3 locations + 1 Tier 5 upgrade differential |
| **Unified dashboard** | Org sees all locations regardless of individual tier | One login, all locations, all tiers visible |

# Competitive Positioning & Market Moat

> *The market is not saturated because the problem required someone willing to do the hard, unglamorous, technically complex work of building a bridge that large retailers never needed and small retailers never knew was possible — until now.*

| Barrier to Entry | Why It Protects Visible Shelf |
|---|---|
| **Engineering complexity** | Clover integration + SEO infrastructure + Google SWIS connectivity + commerce layer is non-trivial to replicate. |
| **Time to build** | A competitor starting today begins months or years behind the existing platform. Head start compounds with every new retailer onboarded. |
| **Google SWIS access** | SWIS is underutilised. Almost no platform has built the bridge between local POS and this channel at scale. |
| **Clover ecosystem** | Clover serves hundreds of thousands of small business merchants. Native Clover integration provides immediate access to an enormous ready-made distribution channel. |
| **Directory network effect** | Every retailer added makes the platform more valuable to shoppers. Every shopper makes the platform more valuable to retailers. |
| **Middle market neglect** | Local retail is too small for enterprise vendors, too complex for website builders, too fragmented for large e-commerce platforms. Visible Shelf owns the gap everyone passed. |
