# Platform Mission
> *Local retailers need a way to move shoppers from real-time product discovery through to purchase conversion because big-box retailers built end-to-end inventory visibility and commerce infrastructure internally — leaving small retailers without an accessible, connected, or affordable path to compete in mobile shopping.*

---

# The Four-Part Problem

| Problem | What It Means for Small Retailers |
|---|---|
| **COMPLEXITY** | Real-time inventory systems require sophisticated technical architecture that small retailers cannot build or maintain. |
| **CONNECTIVITY** | Linking in-store POS inventory to Google Search, Google Shopping, and mobile requires deep integrations across multiple platforms. |
| **AVAILABILITY** | No existing market solution is built specifically for small and independent retailers. Big-box retailers built their own — small retailers have no equivalent path. |
| **AFFORDABILITY** | Even if a solution existed, small retailers cannot sustain the cost of dedicated IT teams or enterprise-level technology investment. |

---

# Platform Architecture

Visible Shelf is a tiered enablement platform connecting local retailer inventory — via Clover POS — to SEO-optimized product pages, branded storefronts, and a public directory. Every layer is powered by the same Clover integration, giving small retailers a complete end-to-end path from product discovery to conversion on mobile and web.

## The Connected Layer Stack

| Layer | Component | Function |
|---|---|---|
| **Integration** | Clover POS Sync | Pulls live inventory into the platform automatically. Single source of truth. |
| **Product** | SEO Product Pages | Each product gets a public, Google-indexed, mobile-optimised page hosted on the platform. |
| **Visibility** | Google Search / Shopping / SWIS | Products surface in Google Search, Google Shopping, and Google Maps See What's In Store in real time. |
| **Storefront** | Public Store Page | Aggregates all products into a branded store experience on the platform. |
| **Directory** | Platform Store Directory | All stores discoverable via search and browse inside the Visible Shelf app and website. |
| **Commerce** | Cart, Checkout & Conversion | Add to cart, commitment checkout with holding deposit, and optional full online payment. |

### Holding Fee Model

| Element | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Fee amount** | 10–15% of order total collected at checkout |
| **On fulfilment** | Credited toward in-store purchase at pickup |
| **On abandonment** | Forfeited if shopper does not collect within 24–48 hour window |
| **Retailer protection** | Inventory never held without financial commitment from shopper |
| **Platform credibility** | Abandonment has financial consequence — prevents hold & abandon at scale |

### Abandoned Fee Distribution

```
Shopper abandons within forfeiture window
↓
Holding fee is forfeited by shopper
↓
Platform deducts processing fee (20–25%)
↓
Retailer receives remainder (75–80%) as compensation
↓
Inventory automatically released
```

| Scenario | Holding Fee | Platform Fee | Retailer Gets |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Order fulfilled** | Credited to purchase | Small processing fee | Full sale revenue |
| **Order abandoned** | Forfeited by shopper | 20–25% of holding fee | 75–80% of holding fee |

The retailer is never the loser. The platform never takes more than it earns. The shopper bears the consequence of their own behaviour.

**Upgrade trigger:** *"Shoppers are reserving and showing up — now I want to close the full sale before they leave the couch."*

# Pricing & Revenue Model

## Pricing Philosophy

Visible Shelf operates in an uncontested market with no natural competition. The engineering investment to replicate the platform — Clover integration, Google SWIS connectivity, SEO infrastructure, and commerce layer — represents a substantial capital and time barrier to entry. A free model would undervalue the platform, under-fund its development, and attract uncommitted retailers.

**Model: 14-day free trial across all tiers. Paid from day 15. No exceptions.**

The 14-day trial is enough time for a retailer to connect Clover, see their products indexed on Google, appear on Google Maps via SWIS, and be listed in the platform directory. The value is visible and measurable within the trial window — making conversion to paid a natural and confident decision, not a leap of faith.

## Platform Revenue Streams

# Organisational Model — Multi-Location

Multi-location businesses — chains, franchises, regional retailers — require a tier model that serves both the organisation as a whole and individual locations with flexibility. Visible Shelf supports two layers: the Organisation Account and the Individual Location.

## The Inheritance & Upgrade Model

```
Organisation Account sets the baseline tier (any of Tier 1–4)
↓
All locations automatically inherit baseline tier features
↓
Individual locations can upgrade above the baseline — never below it
↓
Organisation dashboard provides unified view across all locations and tiers
↓
Billing consolidated at organisation level
```

## Can an Organisation Be Any Tier?

Yes — any of the four tiers, not just Tier 4.

| Org Tier | Organisation Type | Real-World Scenario |
|---|---|---|
| **Tier 1** | Google-first multi-location | Small chain wanting Google visibility across all locations with minimal investment. Each location appears on Google Maps and SWIS immediately. |
| **Tier 2** | Brand-presence organisation | Regional brand wanting a unified storefront presence and product browsability across all locations on the platform. |
| **Tier 3** | Commitment commerce chain | Growing chain wanting reservation and commitment commerce capability deployed uniformly across every location. |
| **Tier 4** | Full e-commerce organisation | Full-scale multi-location retailer wanting end-to-end e-commerce, delivery options, and unified analytics across all locations. |

## Individual Location Flexibility Rules

| Rule | What It Means | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **Downward inheritance** | All locations receive org baseline features automatically | Org on Tier 2 → every location gets storefront and product visibility |
| **Upward flexibility** | Individual locations can upgrade above the org baseline | Org on Tier 2, flagship location upgrades to Tier 4 |
| **No downgrade below baseline** | A location cannot operate below the org tier | Org on Tier 3 → no location can drop to Tier 1 or 2 |
| **Unified billing** | Org pays baseline × locations + individual upgrade differentials | Tier 2 org × 3 locations + 1 Tier 4 upgrade differential |
| **Unified dashboard** | Org sees all locations regardless of individual tier | One login, all locations, all tiers visible |
| **Brand consistency** | All storefronts inherit org brand identity at baseline | Consistent brand look across every location storefront |

## Multi-Location Pricing

| Account Type | Locations | Pricing | Best For |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Single location** | 1 | Standard tier fee | Independent retailers |
| **Enterprise Multi-Location** | 2–5 | $299/mo + $49 per additional location | Small chains, owner-operated multi-site |
| **Enterprise Organisation** | 6–19 | Custom pricing | Regional chains with dedicated support |
| **Enterprise National** | 20+ | Custom + dedicated account management | National or franchise organisations |

## Multi-Location Shopper Experience

When a shopper searches for a product on Visible Shelf or Google, the platform surfaces availability across all nearby locations of the same brand — letting the shopper choose their preferred location and proceed through that location's checkout flow.

```
Shopper searches
↓
Product found at multiple nearby locations
↓
Shopper selects preferred location
↓
Proceeds through that location's checkout flow
↓
Pickup or delivery from chosen location
```

This is a capability that even many large retailers struggle to execute — Visible Shelf delivers it natively through Clover.
